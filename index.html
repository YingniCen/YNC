Hello everyone, My name is Yingni Cen. I was born in China and I am 19 years old. I am a first-year undergraduate student at UC Riverside, which is located in southern California. I am majoring in pre-business major. I know leadership skills are very important in a business major, so I decided to work in a milk tea shop during the summer. Luckily, I worked as an associate manager at a milk tea shop. My leadership skills have significantly improved through this position. Returning customers play an important role in the stable operation of the milk tea shop. Thus, I tried to provide the best service to all customers. I would also monitor my co-workers to do so. I always reminded them that we should stand in the customer's perspective to consider and solve problems, showing sincerity towards customers. But a few weeks later, the shop's monthly sales performance did not improve significantly. One day, a customer walked up to me and asked why the same drink's taste had changed every time. That made me realize the drinks' quality issue, which determines whether your customers would buy back the product. Without stable-taste quality drinks, the store brand's popularity and good local selection are useless, and the store sales will not increase. Therefore, not only good customer service but also the control of product quality is essential.

After that, I arrived early every day and trained the employees to be more familiar with the correct ratio of milk to tea and make sure of the drinks' fresh toppings. Moreover, I asked my teammates to ask the customers about the drinks' taste and use the iPad to jot down the comments on different drinks. Then, I would communicate with the employees every few days to decide whether to adjust the drinks ratio slightly. With the change, the sales volume of drinks increased by the end of that month. In the monthly meeting, we concluded a new point of good customer service: more communication with customers helps cultivate feelings with them and better retain guests.

Through this experience, when I and my team encounter difficulties, I will clarify and understand the problem, split and locate the problem, propose solutions, and take action. I have spent a lot of time considering various career possibilities, and I think this area of work is the best fit for me because it requires many skills that I am good at. For example, problem analysis and problem-solving are my strengths, and when I am on campus I was able to identify and solve problems earlier than others.
